About




I am a data analyst currently located in San Francisco, CA. I earned my bachelor's in cognitive science from Carnegie Mellon University and my master's in psychology from Bucknell University. During this time, I gained both coursework and research experience in Python, Excel, and MATLAB. My research was focused on auditory psychology – specifically, my undergraduate research studied human echolocation, and my master's thesis studied auditory imagery in college students and senior citizens. I presented the results of my work in oral presentations, posters, and written papers.

More recently, I have begun learning about and implementing data analytics techniques for my work. The majority of these I learned while enrolled in a UC Berkeley Extension data analytics/visualization bootcamp, which lasted six months. This bootcamp covered numerous skills such as Pandas, SQL, MongoDB, HTML/CSS, d3, and Tableau.

I currently work as a data analytics intern at Liftopia, an e-commerce company with a focus on lift tickets for ski resorts. Though I knew very little about revenue management in general or the ski industry in particular prior to my internship, I quickly picked up on the many factors that can influence the price of a lift ticket on any given day. I am exposed to data relating to all aspects of the business. For example, I make pricing plans for resorts, incorporating feedback from the resort while also implementing revenue management best practices. Additionally, I analyze revenue and skier visit data for both internal and external purposes. These analyses are performed in SQL, Python, and/or Looker and are visualized in Looker dashboards or Google Sheets. Finally, I am contributing to the development of a model to predict and optimize resort pricing, specifically, determining which resorts are likely to earn a larger-than-average amount of revenue on an upcoming date. I greatly enjoy the process of organizing several disjoint sources of data into actionable insights.
