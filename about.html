About




I am a data analyst currently located in San Francisco, CA. I earned my bachelor's in cognitive science from Carnegie Mellon University and my master's in psychology from Bucknell University. During this time, I gained both coursework and research experience in Python, Excel, and Matlab. My research was focused on auditory psychology – specifically, my undergraduate research studied human echolocation, and my master's thesis studied auditory imagery in college students and senior citizens. I presented the results of my work in oral presentations, posters, and written papers.

More recently, I have begun learning about and implementing data analytics techniques for my work. The majority of these I learned while participating in a UC Berkeley Extension data analytics/visualization bootcamp, which lasted six months. This bootcamp covered numerous skills such as Pandas, SQL, MongoDB, HTML/CSS, d3, and Tableau.

During the bootcamp, I worked as a data analytics intern at Liftopia, an e-commerce company with a focus on lift tickets for ski resorts.
